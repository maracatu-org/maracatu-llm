## Analysis

### Comparison with earlier runs

| Run | Iters | Training tokens | Best val loss | Improvement vs random |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| tiny-v1-gpt2 | 5,000 | ~40M | 6.7514 | 3.0 nats |
| tiny-v2-llama | 5,000 | ~40M | 6.6408 | 3.1 nats |
| **tiny-long** | **50,000** | **~410M** | **6.3949** | **3.3 nats** |

### The key finding: capacity saturation

With 10× more iterations, val loss dropped only **0.25** (from 6.64 to 6.39). In contrast, the first 5000 iters already took the loss from 9.68 (random) to 6.64, a drop of 3.0 nats. Visually on the curve:

- **Steps 0-5,000:** val drops from 9.68 to 6.65 (fast)
- **Steps 5,000-20,000:** val drops from 6.65 to 6.48 (slow)
- **Steps 20,000-50,000:** val goes from 6.48 to 6.39 (almost flat)

This is **capacity saturation**: a 3.2M non-embedding-param model literally has no room to represent more of the corpus. More compute doesn't help. It needs **more parameters**.

### Empirical Chinchilla validation

The tokens-per-parameter ratio landed at **172**, 8.6× above the optimum of 20. The degradation of gain per step is exactly what the law predicts: **to take advantage of a corpus this size, the model should have ~25M non-embedding parameters**, which is roughly where Maracatu-20M fits (17M total ≈ 10.6M non-embedding).

### Qualitative: real and noticeable improvement

Despite the loss only gaining a little in absolute numbers, the samples improved **visibly**:

- Less random "word salad"
- Narrative flows between clauses ("por outro lado", "que já havia sido")
- Real entities appear with contextual coherence: **Rede Globo** in a soap-opera context, Machado + **book + publisher + dates**, Pernambuco + **emperor + king + peace** (historical context, even if factually confused)
- Compound-sentence structure shows up (`..., pela paz em 1798. em 1906, quando ...`)

The nominal loss is only marginally better, but what the model **does** is much better. Reminder that loss isn't the only signal.

### Train/val gap

Stayed small throughout training (< 0.1 nats on the plateau), no detectable overfitting. Makes sense: with 0.7 epoch and weak regularization (dropout=0, weight_decay=0.1), the model had no chance to memorize.

### Implications for the next step (20M)

This experiment validates three decisions:

1. **The Llama-like architecture is correct.** RoPE + SwiGLU + RMSNorm work in our pipeline.
2. **LR/warmup/schedule hyperparameters are well calibrated.** The curve is healthy, no instability, no divergence.
3. **What's missing is parameters, not data nor iteration.** The 20M (~10.6M non-embedding) is exactly the right scale to take advantage of the 550M-token corpus.

### Next step

Move on to the **Maracatu-20M** with no further tiny experimentation. The estimated training time (~6h on M2 or ~3h on a Kaggle T4) is justified by the expected capacity gain: 3.3× more non-embedding parameters should drive val loss to 4.5-5.0 (estimate based on scaling laws).
